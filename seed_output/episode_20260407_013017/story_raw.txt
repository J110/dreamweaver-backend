[CHARACTER: Mira, curious, brave]
[CHARACTER: Tuku, wise, gentle]
[CHARACTER: Lumi, quiet, shimmering]

[INTRO]
Close your eyes and listen. Can you hear the hum of the dream weaver's loom? It's a place where every goodnight wish is spun into silver threads, where lullabies drift like fireflies, and where your very own dream is being packed into a little glowing box just for you. Tonight, the box is ready. Tonight, it's coming to find you.
[/INTRO]

[PHASE_1]
The air smells like warm milk and honey. Mira stands in the middle of the dream weaver's loom, her bare feet sinking into plush moss that glows faintly blue. Above her, the sky is a tapestry of shifting colors — pinks, purples, and golds — like a sunset that never ends. But something is different tonight.

MIRA: "Tuku, did you hear that?"
TUKU: "Hear what, little dreamer?"
MIRA: "A sound. Like... like a box being wrapped."

Tuku is a giant tortoise, his shell etched with ancient patterns that tell stories of every child who's ever slept. He blinks slowly, his eyes soft and knowing.

TUKU: "Perhaps it is your dream, arriving."
MIRA: "But where is it? I don't see anything."

Mira turns in a circle, her heart beating fast. The loom is usually alive with the chatter of dream spinners and the rustle of paper wings. But tonight, everything is still. Too still.

MIRA: "It's too quiet. Something's wrong."
LUMI: "Maybe it's hiding."

Lumi is Mira's shadow — but not like the dark kind. Lumi is made of moonlight, shifting and shimmering beside her, always just a whisper away. Lumi doesn't speak often, but when she does, it's like a secret meant only for Mira.

MIRA: "Hiding? Why would my dream hide?"
LUMI: "Not hiding. Waiting."

Tuku lowers his head, and Mira notices something strange. The patterns on his shell are flickering, like candle flames. Some of the lines are missing.

MIRA: "Tuku, your shell — it's breaking."
TUKU: "Not breaking. Breathing."

He gestures with his nose toward a small, silver key lying on the moss. It wasn't there a moment ago.

TUKU: "Pick it up. But breathe first."

Mira hesitates, then remembers what her mother taught her. In through the nose, slow and deep. Out through the mouth, soft as a whisper. She does it once, twice. The key begins to glow.

[PAUSE: 800]
[BREATHE]

TUKU: "It's warm. [PHRASE]That's your breath, waking it up.[/PHRASE]"

The key fits perfectly into a tiny lock on Tuku's shell. When Mira turns it, the missing lines reappear, brighter than before. A soft chime rings out, and the air hums with a new sound — like a box being tied with ribbon.

TUKU: "Mira. The dream is coming. It's almost here."

The moss beneath Mira's feet begins to vibrate. Tiny lights flicker in the distance, like fireflies dancing in a line. But they're not fireflies. There's something else.

MIRA: "Tuku, what are those?"
TUKU: "Dream carriers. They've been searching for you."

The lights grow brighter, pulsing in time with Mira's heartbeat. The hum of the loom swells into a melody, soft and sweet. Like a lullaby sung by a hundred voices, the world itself is singing. The music wraps around Mira, warm and gentle. She closes her eyes and lets it carry her for a moment. When she opens them, the lights are closer. So close she can almost touch them.

[SONG_SEED: A lullaby about a child waiting for their dream to find them, like fireflies leading the way home.]
[/PHASE_1]

[POST_SONG]
The melody fades, but the warmth remains. The dream weaver's loom is quieter now, as if it's holding its breath. The fireflies have settled into a soft glow around Mira's feet, and the air smells like lavender and sleep. Something is changing. Something is arriving.
[/POST_SONG]

[PHASE_2]
Mira sits down on the moss, her legs crossed. The glow from the fireflies is soothing, like a nightlight made of stars. Lumi curls up beside her, her moonlight edges flickering softly.

LUMI: "I'm sleepy."
MIRA: "Already? But the dream isn't here yet."

Lumi doesn't answer. Her glow dims, then stills — like a candle being blown out. Mira watches her, waiting for her to move, but Lumi is perfectly still. Mira reaches out and touches her. Lumi's light is warm, but she doesn't stir.

MIRA: "Tuku, is Lumi okay?"
TUKU: "She's resting. Dreams need quiet to arrive."

Mira looks at Tuku. His eyes are half closed, his breathing slow and steady. The patterns on his shell are shifting again, but this time they're fading, like ink in water.

MIRA: "Tuku?"

His voice is softer now, like he's speaking from far away.

MIRA: "Are you going to sleep too?"
TUKU: "Soon. But not yet."

[PAUSE: 1500]
[BREATHE]

Mira nods, even though she's not sure he can see her. She takes a deep breath — in through her nose, out through her mouth. The key in her hand glows faintly, pulsing like a heartbeat. The fireflies around her feet are still, their light steady and calm. The air is warmer now, like a blanket fresh from the dryer.

Mira lies back on the moss, her head cradled in her hands. The sky above her is shifting again, but slower this time, like it's stretching before a nap. The colors are softer — blues and grays, like twilight.

[PHRASE]The dream is coming. The dream is coming.[/PHRASE]

She whispers it to herself, her voice barely there. The key in her hand grows heavier. But not in a bad way. It's like it's settling into her palm, becoming part of her. She closes her fingers around it and feels something new — a quiet, a stillness, a knowing.

The fireflies around her begin to dim, fading into the moss. The hum of the loom is gone, replaced by a sound like distant waves, or maybe the rhythm of her own breath. In through the nose, out through the mouth. Slow. Steady.

[PAUSE: 1500]
[BREATHE]

Mira's eyelids grow heavy. She blinks once, twice, and then they stay closed. The warmth of the moss seeps into her back, her arms, her legs. It's like being wrapped in a hug from the world itself.

She can still hear the waves, but they're softer now, like a whisper. Tuku's breathing is deep and slow beside her. She can feel it, like a gentle tide pulling her further into the quiet.

The key in her hand is no longer glowing. It's just a key now, warm and still. She lets it go, and it sinks into the moss without a sound.

The last thing Mira feels is the air on her skin, cool and soft, like a kiss goodnight. The last thing she hears is the quiet — the stillness — the peace. It's not a sound, not really. It's the absence of sound, the space between breaths, the moment before sleep.
