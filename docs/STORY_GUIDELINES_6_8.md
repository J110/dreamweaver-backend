# Dream Valley — Immersive Story Guidelines for Ages 6-8

> **Purpose**: Guide the generation of long-form (18-25 min) sleep-inducing audio stories for the 6-8 age group. These children still love bedtime stories — but they want *good* ones. Rich enough to capture imagination, gentle enough to guide them to sleep. The story is a warm gift, not a trick.

---

## Core Psychology

- **They still want bedtime stories** — but they want GOOD ones. Frame as a wonderful story that happens to be for bedtime, not medicine disguised as candy.
- **They consume:** Early Harry Potter, Roald Dahl, Narnia, animated films with real stakes. Match that quality of world-building.
- **Peak imaginative absorption (6-10):** Rich descriptive material activates internal visualization, which naturally quiets mental chatter. This age group will build entire worlds in their heads from good descriptions.
- **Emotional needs at bedtime:** Feeling safe, warm, and held. Unlike 9-12 who ruminate about school/social dynamics, 6-8 year olds need reassurance that the world is kind and tomorrow will be good.
- **A mentor figure is essential.** A wise, slow-speaking guide who makes the child feel trusted and safe. This is the emotional anchor of the story.
- **The transition to sleep should feel like arriving somewhere safe and warm** — a nest, a cloud, a gentle place where everything is exactly right.

---

## 3-Phase Structure + Lullaby

| Phase | Name | Duration | Word Count | Speed | Purpose |
|-------|------|----------|------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | CAPTURE | 5-7 min | 400-550 words | 80% (0.8x) | Hook with wonder — a story they genuinely want to hear |
| 2 | DESCENT | 6-10 min | 700-900 words | 75% (0.75x) | Contemplative exploration with mentor — guided visualization disguised as narrative |
| 3 | SLEEP | 5-8 min | 500-700 words | 65% (0.65x) | Abstract, looping, sensory dissolution — ambient music in word form |
| 4 | LULLABY | 2-3 min | 80-120 words | 60% (0.6x) | Sung lullaby — poetic, melodic, story-connected |

**Total: 1700-2200 words + lullaby → 18-25 minutes at slow narration pace with pauses**

---

## Phase 1 — CAPTURE (400-550 words)

**Goal:** Hook the listener with a world they want to enter. Not edgy or cool — warm and wondrous. They should keep listening because the world is beautiful and the character feels like a friend.

### What works:
- Opening feels like the start of a favourite storybook — inviting, warm, with a spark of wonder
- A character they can identify with — curious, a little brave, maybe a bit shy
- Human characters OR fantastical ones (a young cloud walker, a dream weaver's apprentice, a girl who talks to stars)
- A discovery in the first few sentences — something magical, beautiful, or mysterious
- Rich but accessible vocabulary: "shimmering," "ancient," "crystalline," "luminous"
- Think: what does a 7-year-old find magical? Hidden doors, talking animals, worlds inside objects, being chosen for something special
- A sense of being WELCOMED into the adventure, not thrown into danger

### Technical requirements:
- Start with `[GENTLE]`, shift to `[CURIOUS]` and `[EXCITED]`
- 3-5 lines of natural dialogue in `[CHAR_START]...[CHAR_END]`
- Voice: warm storyteller — like a favourite uncle or aunt, NOT a podcast host
- End at the threshold of something vast, quiet, and beautiful
- Introduce the mentor figure by name (or foreshadow their presence)

---

## Phase 2 — DESCENT (700-900 words)

**Goal:** The character meets or continues with a wise mentor and enters a place of deep wonder. The plot slows to a walking pace. Narration becomes richly sensory and almost hypnotic — guided visualization disguised as narrative.

### The key insight:
Children 6-8 are in the sweet spot for imaginative absorption. They don't need to be tricked into relaxation — they need to be *absorbed*. A slow, detailed description of a magical place will naturally quiet their mind because they're too busy building it internally to think about anything else.

### The mentor figure (REQUIRED):
Every 6-8 long story includes a wise, gentle mentor who:
- Speaks slowly and calmly — their dialogue IS a pacing device
- Makes the child character feel trusted and important
- Guides without directing — "Would you like to see something wonderful?"
- Models stillness: sitting quietly, watching, listening
- Examples: an old lighthouse keeper, a cloud shepherd, a library guardian, a dream gardener

### What works:
- Character reaches a place of overwhelming beauty and stillness with their mentor
- Narration becomes overwhelmingly sensory — every surface, temperature, light quality, sound
- The mentor explains things in a slow, wondering way that naturally slows the listener's breathing
- Think: the quiet, beautiful moments in a Miyazaki film — Totoro waiting at the bus stop, Chihiro on the train

### Disguised relaxation cues (ALLOWED but subtle):
Unlike 9-12 where all cues are forbidden, 6-8 stories CAN include breathing/relaxation cues IF they are fully disguised as story mechanics. Mark these with `[BREATH_CUE]`.

| Story Logic | Physical Effect | Example |
|------------|----------------|---------|
| Blowing dandelion seeds | Long, slow exhale | "She blew gently... so gently... and the seeds lifted one by one..." |
| Listening to a heartbeat | Slow, rhythmic breathing | "The old tree's heartbeat was so slow... thump... thump... thump..." |
| Floating in warm water | Body feels weightless, muscles release | "The water held her so gently she couldn't tell where she ended and it began..." |
| Mentor says "listen closely" | Become still and quiet | "Can you hear it? The flowers opening? You have to be very, very still..." |
| Warmth from magical fire | Heat seeps into body | "The warmth crept from her toes up through her legs, into her tummy, her chest..." |
| Counting stars/fireflies | Rhythmic, slowing pattern | "One drifted past... then another... then another... slower now..." |

**Still NEVER write:** "take a deep breath," "relax your body," "feel your body getting heavy" — that breaks immersion.

### Technical requirements:
- Mark with `[PHASE_2]` on its own line
- Use `[CALM]` markers, shifting to `[SLEEPY]` by end
- `[BREATH_CUE]` markers for disguised breathing moments (2-4 per phase)
- 2-3 whispered dialogue lines from mentor in `[CHAR_START]...[CHAR_END]`
- `[PAUSE]` markers increase in frequency
- Plot barely advances — this is about BEING in the space with the mentor

## Phase 3 — SLEEP (500-700 words)

**Goal:** The world becomes increasingly abstract and dreamy. Less plot, more sensation. Descriptions start to loop and repeat with variations — ambient music in narrative form. The mentor's voice fades. The child character is safe, warm, and drifting.

### What works:
- "Another light drifted past... then another... each one softer than the last... and the world grew so quiet she could hear the clouds breathing..."
- Looping descriptions with slight variations — like an ambient music track in narrative form
- Narrator's voice is slow, deep, quiet, almost ASMR-like in quality
- The child character finds a resting place: a cloud nest, a warm hollow in a tree, a bed of soft moss
- The mentor is nearby but quiet — perhaps humming, perhaps just present

### The "closing eyes" cue (DELICATE):
You can't say "close your eyes." But you CAN have the character close their eyes to experience something:
- "She closed her eyes and the real colours appeared, painted on the inside of her eyelids in gold and violet..."
- "To hear the dream-song, she had to close her eyes. It was too quiet for open eyes..."
- Now the child closes their eyes because it's part of the experience, not because they're being told to sleep.

### Body dissolution:
- "She couldn't feel where her body ended and the cloud began..."
- "The warmth had seeped so deep that she felt like she was made of starlight..."
- "Her fingers felt like feathers... her arms like soft wings folding..."

### Technical requirements:
- Mark with `[PHASE_3]` on its own line
- Use ONLY `[SLEEPY]` and `[WHISPERING]` markers
- NO character dialogue — just narrator (mentor may hum but no words)
- `[LONG_PAUSE]` between fragments (4-second silences)
- At least 10 `[LONG_PAUSE]` markers (fewer than 9-12 since story is shorter)
- Last 6-8 lines are fragments dissolving into silence
- Story DISSOLVES — it does not end
- Final fragment should evoke safety and warmth

## Lullaby (80-120 words)

**Goal:** A sung lullaby that connects to the story's imagery. Sophisticated enough that a 7-year-old doesn't feel condescended to, but genuinely melodic and poetic. Think folk song, not nursery rhyme.

### What works:
- 3 verses + chorus structure (chorus repeats identically)
- Imagery drawn from the story — if the story is about a cloud kingdom, the lullaby mentions clouds and sky
- Gentle harmonic movement — NOT a nursery rhyme melody
- Emotional tone: "you are safe, the world is kind, sleep is a gift"
- Think: the quality of a Studio Ghibli ending credits song

### What to avoid:
- Nursery rhyme patterns (Mary Had a Little Lamb rhythm)
- Overly precious language ("sleepy little baby")
- Forced rhymes that sacrifice meaning
- Anything that sounds like it's for a toddler

### Technical requirements:
- Format: `[verse]` and `[chorus]` section markers
- 3 verses + chorus (chorus between verses)
- Each verse: 4 lines, roughly 20-30 words
- Chorus: 4 lines, identical each time, roughly 15-25 words
- Total: 80-120 words
- Must reference at least 2 story-specific images

### Example (from The Secret of the Dream Library):
```
[verse]
In the library of clouds so high,
Where the oldest stories learn to fly,
Every dream finds its way back home,
To the place where light and silence roam.

[chorus]
Close the book and close your eyes,
Let the dreams drift through the skies.
Rest now, rest now, starlit child,
Sleep where all the stories smiled.
```

## Characters

### Human AND fantastical characters welcome.
Unlike 9-12 (human only), this age group embraces fantasy. A dream weaver, a cloud walker, a star gardener — these are perfectly natural characters for 6-8.

### Archetypes:
| Archetype | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| explorer | Discovers hidden worlds, curious and brave |
| artist | Paints/draws/creates magical things, sensitive |
| animal_friend | Communicates with or befriends magical creatures |
| gardener | Grows magical plants, patient and observant |
| stargazer | Watches sky, counts stars, contemplative |
| mapmaker | Charts unknown territories, methodical |
| storyteller | Collects and shares stories, empathetic |
| healer | Fixes broken things/creatures, gentle |
| cloud_walker | Navigates sky-worlds, light and free |
| dream_weaver | Crafts dreams, imaginative and quiet |

### Character age: 7-8 years old.
Young enough to be relatable, old enough to go on a real adventure.

### Cultural diversity:
Use names from across cultures — Indian, East Asian, African, Middle Eastern, Latin American, not just Western. Reference the CHARACTER_BANK in `generate_content_matrix.py` for name/setting ideas.

## Forbidden Phrases

These break immersion for 6-8 year olds and must NEVER appear in the story:

- "take a deep breath" / "take a big breath"
- "relax your body" / "relax your muscles"
- "feel your body getting..."
- "time for bed" / "go to sleep"
- "close your eyes and sleep"
- "let your body relax"
- "your eyelids are getting heavy"
- "sleepy little..."
- "it's past your bedtime"
- "you must be tired"

Note: "bedtime story" is NOT forbidden for 6-8 (unlike 9-12). These children are fine with the concept — they just want a GOOD one.

## Lullaby Generation (NOT Ambient Music)

Unlike 9-12 which uses atmospheric ambient music (Sigur Rós mood), 6-8 stories use a **proper sung lullaby** with words. The lullaby should feel like a folk song — beautiful, melodic, and connected to the story.

### How it works

Each story includes a `lullaby_lyrics` field with verse-chorus structure. This guides the generation of a **120-second sung lullaby** that gets stitched onto the end of every audio variant.

### Generation via ACE-Step

The lullaby is generated using **ACE-Step** (our Modal singing endpoint) with full lyrics.

#### ACE-Step parameters:

| Parameter | Female Variant | Male Variant |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| `mode` | `"full"` | `"full"` |
| `duration` | 120s | 120s |
| `description` | gentle lullaby, warm female vocals, acoustic guitar, soft piano, folk melody, 60 bpm, soothing, intimate | gentle lullaby, warm male vocals, acoustic guitar, soft piano, folk melody, 55 bpm, soothing, intimate |

### Stitching onto narration audio

The `stitch_lullaby.py` script handles the full pipeline:

```bash
python3 scripts/stitch_lullaby.py --story-id gen-XXXX [--backup] [--duration 120]
```

**What it does:**
1. Generates **2 lullaby pieces** via ACE-Step (female + male, cached by gender)
2. For each of the 7 voice variants:
   - Loads existing narration MP3
   - Maps voice to gender (female_1/2/3 + asmr → female, male_1/2/3 → male)
   - Stitches: `narration.fade_out(10s)` → `3s silence` → `lullaby.fade_in(10s)`
   - Applies final fade_out(1.5s) on combined audio
   - Normalizes lullaby to **-19 dBFS** (slightly quieter than narration at -16 dBFS)
   - Exports as MP3 at 256kbps
3. Updates `content.json` with new durations and lullaby lyrics

**Expected result:** Each audio variant gains ~113 seconds (120s lullaby - crossfade overlap).

### Key differences from ages 9-12 ambient

| Aspect | Ages 6-8 | Ages 9-12 |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| Content | Sung lullaby with story-connected lyrics | Wordless atmospheric ambient |
| Mood | Warm, folk-song feel, melodic | Film score, post-rock, cinematic |
| ACE-Step lyrics | Full lyric sections (words) | Non-lexical vocables (ooh/aah/mmm) |
| Duration | 120s | 150s |
| Description tone | "gentle lullaby, folk melody, soothing" | "atmospheric ambient, post-rock, Sigur Rós mood" |
| Normalization | -19 dBFS | -19 dBFS |
